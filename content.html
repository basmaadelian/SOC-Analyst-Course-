🎓 What Just Happened — Social Engineering Analysis

TRIGGER #1 — URGENCY

The flashing red bar, the live timer, and the blinking dots were all designed to make you feel that something catastrophic is happening right now and you must act immediately. Urgency disables rational thinking — exactly as Mitnick describes in The Art of Deception, Chapter 1.

TRIGGER #2 — FEAR

Seeing your "files being stolen," your "passwords captured," and your "systems infected" creates panic. A panicked user does not verify sources — they click whatever link the attacker provides next. This is why ransomware screens and fake virus alerts are so effective.

TRIGGER #3 — AUTHORITY

Technical jargon (C2 server, SMB scan, HKCU\Run, lateral movement), realistic-looking graphs, and official-sounding log entries all signal legitimacy. Mitnick: an attacker who uses internal terminology is believed without question because only insiders know those terms.

TRIGGER #4 — SPECIFICITY

Specific file names, exact MB sizes, real-looking IP addresses, and named locations make the attack feel personal and targeted. The more specific the detail, the more the target believes it must be real. This is the core of spear phishing — fabricated specificity.
